Ice cream

  • Subtitle

    Ice cream is a mixture of milk, cream, sugar, and sometimes other ingredients that has been frozen into a soft, creamy delight using special techniques.

  • Subtitle

    Ice cream has been a popular treat for hundreds of years but has only become commonplace since the widespread use of refrigeration.

  • Subtitle

    A rich, sweet, creamy frozen food made from variously flavored cream and milk products churned or stirred to a smooth consistency during the freezing process and often containing gelatin, eggs, fruits, nuts, etc.

Ice coffee

  • Subtitle

    An iced coffee is a cold version of your favourite coffee, but it's not simply a coffee that has been left to cool. Like a regular coffee, it's usually a combination of hot espresso and milk. However, ice is added too.

  • Subtitle

    The product of iced coffee is exactly the same as hot coffee, only that it goes through a cooling process. However, when you pour your coffee into ice it can tend to lose flavor, due to the ice melting.

  • Subtitle

    Drinking iced coffee is a real refreshment compared to hot coffee. You'll never burn your tongue on that really hot coffee again.

Milkshake

  • Subtitle

    It is usually prepared by milk, ice cream or iced milk, emulsifier and/or stabilizer, and flavorings or sweeteners (e.g., fruit syrup or chocolate sauce). Milkshake contains ice cream unlike milk smoothie.

  • Subtitle

    Full-service restaurants, ice cream shops, soda fountains, and diners usually prepare the shake in a milkshake machine. Milkshakes may be made from any flavor of ice cream; additional flavorings, such as chocolate syrup, malt syrup, or malted milk powder.

  • Subtitle

    A milkshake (sometimes simply called a shake) is a sweet beverage made by blending milk, ice cream, and flavorings or sweeteners such as butterscotch, caramel sauce, chocolate syrup, fruit syrup, or whole fruit into a thick, sweet, cold mixture.
